### Case Study: The "Reverse-Payment" UPI Scam



##### Type of Cybercrime: UPI / Online Payment Fraud



**Definition and Overview**

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of social engineering where a criminal tricks a victim into authorizing a transaction that debits money from their own account. Unlike traditional hacking, which targets software vulnerabilities, this crime targets human psychology—specifically the victim's lack of technical familiarity with "Request Money" features on apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or BHIM.



**The Step-by-Step Execution**



1. The Initial Contact: The scammer identifies a victim on a secondary marketplace (such as OLX or Facebook Marketplace). They pose as a motivated buyer for an item the victim has listed.



2\.Building Trust: To "secure" the item, the scammer offers to send an advance payment. They often use a fake sense of urgency or claim to be a busy professional or army personnel to build unearned trust.



3\. The Deception: The scammer sends a "Collect Request" (a notification asking for money) instead of a payment. They call the victim and provide a false instruction: "I have sent the payment; you just need to tap 'Pay' and enter your UPI PIN to 'verify' and receive the funds into your bank.



4\. The Theft: The moment the victim enters their 4 or 6-digit UPI PIN, they are legally authorizing a debit from their account. The funds are instantly transferred to the scammer’s untraceable account.



**Target Audience and Consequences**



The primary targets are university students, small-scale entrepreneurs, and senior citizens who are relatively new to digital banking. The immediate consequence is financial loss, often ranging from ₹5,000 to ₹50,000. Beyond the monetary theft, victims face "digital trauma," leading to a loss of confidence in the digital economy and significant stress when dealing with bank recovery processes, which are often difficult once a PIN has been voluntarily entered.



**Ambassador’s Security Rule:** 



You never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If a transaction asks for a PIN, money is leaving your account.
